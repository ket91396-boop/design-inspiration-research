# Quality Rubric

Load this when selecting final references and assigning `source_quality`, `quality_tier`, and `copyright_risk`.

## Source Quality

- `primary`: source is a strong fit for the category and likely authored/curated close to the original work.
- `secondary`: useful adjacent source or discovery source, but not ideal as main evidence.
- `supplement`: weak or contextual source. Use sparingly, mostly for anti-patterns or market scan notes.

## Quality Tier

- `green`: borrow abstract design logic safely: composition, palette ratio, spacing, material, hierarchy, interaction pattern, photographic treatment.
- `yellow`: strong reference but needs transformation: recognizable campaign, branded system, distinct custom lettering, licensed photo, highly specific illustration.
- `red`: avoid or treat as warning: template-market output, obvious IP, trademark-like mark, copied mascot style, generic stock layout, overused trope.

## Copyright Risk

- `low`: generic design principle, public-facing source used only for abstract inspiration, no exact asset reuse.
- `medium`: distinctive composition, custom type, commercial photo, identifiable campaign, or source with unclear license.
- `high`: logo/mark similarity, copyrighted character, proprietary illustration, recognizably copied layout, template/source asset reuse.

## Selection Rules

- Final primary references should be mostly `green` or `yellow`.
- Include `red` only to explain what to avoid.
- Every reference must say what to borrow and what not to copy.
- Every direction should cite the reference patterns behind it, not just a mood adjective.
- For HTML reports, missing `image` is acceptable when the reference page quality is high.
